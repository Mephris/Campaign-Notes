So basically will aim for learning dragon age tarot style




- **The Paper Overlay:** Download a high-res "Cold Press Paper" or "Vintage Parchment" texture. Set the layer mode to **Multiply** at 30% opacity.
    
- **The Noise Layer:** Add a layer of "Perlin Noise" or "Grain." It should be barely visible but makes the digital art look like a physical print.
    
- **The Linear Burn:** Do your line work in a **Dark Brown** ($HEX: \#3b2a1e$), never pure black. Set that layer to **Linear Burn**. This makes the lines look like they are "soaked" into the paper.
  
  
  
  
  - **"Alphonse Mucha Tutorial":** The Dragon Age style is heavily inspired by **Art Nouveau**. Learning how he used thick outlines and flat colors will give you the exact technical foundation you need.
    
- **"Geometric Silhouette Concept Art":** This will teach you how to make an asset look "cool" and "powerful" with very few actual lines.
    
- **Nick Thornborrow:** He is the lead architect of that style. Just looking at his portfolio for 10 minutes is a masterclass in how to simplify a complex idea into a tarot card.
