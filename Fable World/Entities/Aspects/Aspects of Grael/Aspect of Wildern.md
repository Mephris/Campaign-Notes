## Nature & Domains

Wildern is the first Aspect to walk the world, born the moment [[Grael]]'s light fell upon formless earth and the first root took hold. She is not a goddess of nature in any nurturing sense — she is nature as it was _before_ it learned that imperfection has beauty. Cold, vast, and consuming.

Her domain is the untamed wilderness in its most absolute form: not wild as in free and beautiful, but wild as in **empty and uncompromising**. She is the logic of perfect growth, and the terror of what happens when that logic meets a world that refuses to be perfect.

She presides over the sunless age — a world of ink-flooded nights, lightless days, and living woods grown from discarded ichor. A world she tried endlessly to cleanse of the staining, shifting ink of [[Tuatha]], and failed.

**Associated domains:** Wilderness, growth, purity, consumption, the sunless world, the blind beasts of night, the Loom.

## The Lesson

Wildern did not survive her age. She was killed by [[Edal — Aspect of Defiance|Edal]], a mortal she had used, whose children she had taken, and whose grief she had underestimated. Her skull split. Her world burned. The ink she had spent an age trying to wash away became the colorful clouds that light the night sky.

Her ashes were carried down from the peak of the world by rivers — down into the low, stained, imperfect places she had spent her existence trying to cleanse.

There, she remembered.

She remembered her own death. She remembered the shape of her cruelty — the loom, the taken children, the blind beast given a woman's face. And in the age that followed, as she lay in pieces along river banks and forest floors, broken and formless, it was one of the wood elves she had tried to destroy who extended her help. An imperfect being. A stained one. One she would have unmade without hesitation.

She accepted the help.

In that acceptance was the whole of her transformation. The goddess who built a world to be perfect learned, through dying and being carried by rivers and being helped by something she had despised, that **the stain was never the corruption. The stain was the point.**

She grew back. Not as Wildern — not the cold She-Owl who watched from unreachable heights and found everything below her wanting — but as the **Wild Mother**: nature lined with beauty, wilderness that shelters as fiercely as it devours, a goddess who finally came down from the tree and let her imperfect children meet her eyes.
