# Note
Humans for a long time were considered the rats of the world, the witherblooms, among all mortal species they are the shortest living race. 

Only after events of the Dragon war against [[Valka]] they gained some traction as they were the ones who actually pushed back the dragoness back to the life's cliff. 

Humans in this setting are:
- Shortest living race
- For a long time considered a lesser race that spread rapidly among the other lands. 
- They were born from [[Conflict]] wish to solve a problem - A race of problem solvers. 
- All the Dakr are bound to Carnelian Commonwealth, the commonwealth is their only home land. 
- Humans across all world consider themselves Carnelian citizens and will therefore often answer the call of arms of Carnelian, it also makes the commonwealth extremely stable to destabilization as there is no real discrepancy the larger the commonwealth gets, there are squabbles between nobles but nothing serious. 
- Carnelians are most friendly and loyal race, if you call onto them they will come to help you as if your people were their own. 
- If you push Carnelian patience too far, their ideological cruelty kicks in, they will give you chance to evacuate before slaughtering everything that is left, considering it a problem of the creature... could have reincarnated better then as a horse, life stock or a child. 
- Carnelians are hard to conquer, if you take carnelian land you have to pretty much do a population cull to remove humans from the land which most wont do... most...are not Lune Magocracy. 


## Wordbuilding
Dakr is the original name of the Speacies used by Dakr to speak about themselves, meaning "Sharp Tear"

> [!note] **Dakr – Dakrans**
> Derived from the **Proto-Indo-European (PIE)** root ***dakru-** (pronounced /ˈda.kru/).
>  
> **PIE Core:** From the root ***dak-** ("to bite" or "sharp") + ***aru** ("flow"), describing the "stinging/sharp" sensation of a salt-tear.  
> **Original Meaning:** "Tear" or "shred of weeping."  

However most races use the second bit as both a slur and term to speak about the Dakr as the spill, the mistake of an aspect. Wasted/Spilled Thought, Human. 

> [!note] **Humon – Ghuman**
> A compound of two **Proto-Indo-European (PIE)** roots: ***ǵhu-** and ***-mōn**.
> 
> **Prefix *ǵhu-:** From the root ***ǵheu-** ("to pour" or "to flow"). The same root used for sacred libations and the ancient origin of the word _God_.  
> **Suffix *-mōn:** From the core root ***men-** ("to think," "mind," or "spirit"). Found in Latin _mens_ and Sanskrit _manas_.  
> **Original Meaning:** "The Thinking Flow" or "The Poured Mind."
