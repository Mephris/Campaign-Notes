Around the 10th century Lugh's ancestor was a thief that disturbed an ancient corpse, because of this ever since all of his line was possesed by the Draugr spirit. Slowly making the family become more and more knowledgeable in the ways of breaking their curses and traveling the world for some artifact that could remove the possession. 

In the 19th century, Lugh grew up trying to study and help people ailed by the curses and ailments. As his father died the revenant passed onto him, turning the helpful and bright person into more of an grumpy drunkard.

He still is himself, he wants to help people and communicate with the vaesen not even out of the need of breaking the curse but thats what he liked to do beforehand and if one of the traits amplified was stubbornness he will god damn continue to do it.
