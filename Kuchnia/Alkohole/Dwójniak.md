# Spiced Apple Mead

**Type:** Rapid-Aged Mead Variant (Dwójniak Style)
**Profile:** Deep Amber, Viscous, Warming, "Phantom" Heat
**Yield:** Approx. 1 Liter
**Prep Time:** 1 Hour Active | 48 Hours Passive

---

## Description
This recipe is designed to mimic the depth and complexity of a traditional barrel-aged *Dwójniak* Mead in a fraction of the time. By using specific botanical heat sources (ginger, pepper) and acid adjustments (vinegar, lemon), it creates a drink that tastes significantly older than it is, bypassing the months of fermentation usually required.

---

## Ingredients

### The Liquid Base
* **Spirits:** ~400ml Total (Blend of Dark Rum, Vodka, and Bourbon recommended for complexity).
* **Water:** 200ml.

### The Sweetness (Body)
* **Base Honey:** 350g (Flowery or Multi-flower honey).
* **Finishing Honey:** 2–3 tbsp (High-quality Raw Honey).

### The Aromatics (Flavor Structure)
* **Green Apple:** 1 whole, sliced into wedges (skin left on).
* **Cinnamon:** 1 stick (shattered/broken).
* **Ginger:** 1 small disc (fresh, peeled).
* **Black Peppercorns:** 2 whole (cracked).
* **Lemon:** 1 strip of yellow zest + 1 tbsp fresh juice.
* **Apple Vinegar:** 1–2 tsp (to simulate fermentation acidity).
* **Salt:** ~1/8 tsp (dissolved).

---

## Instructions

### Phase 1: The Decoction (The Base)
1.  **Extract Tannins:** In a saucepan, combine **water**, **apple slices**, and the broken **cinnamon stick**. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer gently for 10–15 minutes.
2.  **Create Syrup:** Remove from heat. While still hot, stir in the **350g of base honey**. Stir until completely dissolved.
3.  **Cool:** Let the mixture cool until it is warm to the touch (approx. 40°C). Do not add alcohol while boiling.

### Phase 2: The Fortification & Infusion
1.  **Combine:** Pour the warm syrup into a large glass jar. Add your **Spirits** (Rum/Vodka/Bourbon blend) and the **Apple Vinegar**.
2.  **Add Aromatics:** Add the **Ginger disc**, cracked **Peppercorns**, and **Lemon Peel**.
    * *Note on Cloves:* If adding cloves, monitor closely. Remove after 12 hours to prevent numbing.
3.  **Macerate:** Seal the jar tight. Store in a dark, cool place for **36–48 hours**. Shake occasionally.

### Phase 3: The Rescue & Polish (The Finish)
1.  **Strain:** After 48 hours, strain the liquid through a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth. Discard all solids (apples, spices, peels).
2.  **Acid Check:** Taste. If it is too cloying, add the **1 tbsp Lemon Juice**.
3.  **The "Salt Hack":** Dissolve **1/8 tsp of salt** in a small shot of the mead, then pour it back into the main bottle. This neutralizes bitterness from the apple skins.
4.  **Back-Sweetening:** In a separate cup, dissolve the **2–3 tbsp of Raw Honey** into a small amount of the mead. Pour this back into the main bottle. This restores the floral fresh-honey aroma lost during cooking.

---

## Service & Storage

* **Storage:** Keep at room temperature or slightly chilled. The flavors will harmonize (marry) further after bottling.
* **Serving Style:**
    * **Neat:** Serve in a small glass at room temperature.
    * **The "Honey Rim":** For the best experience, rub a drop of fresh honey on the rim of the glass before serving. This olfactory trick maximizes the perceived sweetness and masks any lingering bitterness.
