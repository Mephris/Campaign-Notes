Born from the first act of deliberate destruction - not in anger or need, but simply to see something cease to exist - the Unmaker appears as a child-like figure whose touch causes things to slowly unravel. Not violently, but gently, as if they were never meant to exist at all. It offers visitors the gift of undoing their greatest mistakes, but the price is always something more precious than what is restored.

In reality, Unmaker is a blade from a time long gone, owned by the predecessor of the [[Aspect of Conflict]]. It takes on a form of a child to wield itself, while taking away guilt and empathy of others that wield it.
