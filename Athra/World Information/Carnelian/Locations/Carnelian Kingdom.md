Across the fertile lowlands where five realms meet stands a kingdom that should not exist—Carnelian, the nation forged by briefest and most determined race. What once began as a foolish dream of a merchant has grown into a testament to human resilience, a civilization that has transformed the very curse of their brief lives into their greatest weapon.

Once called “witherblooms” by the elves and “sparks” by the dwarves, humans were seen as little more than fleeting moments in the histories of long-lived races. Their short lives made them perpetual nomads, always the outsiders, never truly belonging anywhere. Yet in this very brevity, a merchant dreamer found humanity’s greatest strength—the ability to adapt and evolve faster than any other race.

The landscape of Carnelian is shaped by pragmatic ingenuity: rolling plains converted to fields that can feed both citizen and soldier, granaries and baraks in large cities able to hold all surrounding villages, and rivers strategically redirected to serve both commerce and defense. At the borders of the cities stand large wooden watchtowers, with the standard of a ram horned pheanix in the golden fields.

At the heart of this kingdom lies the city bearing the kingdoms name. Once a village in the midst of barbaric lands, now a crimson jewel holding the royal palace, high temples and the Aspect of Eternal War themselves.

At the center of all political and religious struggles lies the Firebird, Carnelian Pheanix, the Aspect of Eternal Conflict made mortal to live and fight together with its brief kin. Standing as a beacon of hope and the catastrphic deterent to neighboring nations.

On the north, the High elven peaks and their Aspect of Ambition, Avia, right below them is the kingdom of Dwarvenkin with their Aspect of Amity, Durh

Where elves contemplate strategies for centuries, Carnelians test and reinvent a dozen approaches in a single human lifetime. Where dwarves build to endure ages, Carnelians construct with focus to adaptation rather then permamence. Where fey weave ancient magics, Carnelians cold-smith weapons to sever such enchantments.

When Carnelian’s borders are threatened beyond tolerance, a sight few outsiders have witnessed unfolds—the smoke from a thousand clerical censers darkens the sky, forge-hammers ring without ceasing, and every citizen becomes part of a war machine that has, against all odds, pushed back the borders of immortal kingdoms and carved out a place where the shortest-lived race has built a legacy that grows stronger with each fleeting generation passed.
