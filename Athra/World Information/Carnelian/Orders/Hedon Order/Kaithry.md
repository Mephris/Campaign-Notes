### The Clan of Kaithry

_“I apologize for the late hour, but I heard you were sick?.  
I have some small skill with medicine, if you would allow it.  
I ask no payment–only the chance to help, if I may.”_

While Hedon is the dark fist that serves Carnelian in its darkest ends, there are its members that have long forgone their cruelty together with their mortality. The oldest of Bloodworn are often found leaving the clan to become what Hedon Knights call “Kaithry.”

Kaithry are wanderers who have forgone their axe and sword in favor of scalpel and needle instead. These physicians travel Carnelian lands and beyond to heal and treat the mortal folk. They are a sign of good fortune which comes to any village they pass through—the sick become healthy, the weak grow in strength, while the shy gain confidence. They are learned healers who never seek recompense for their services, often turning whatever gifts they receive into tools to help them heal others.

Unlike other Orders with halls and hierarchies, the Kaithry walk alone. They form no brotherhood, claim no territory, and follow no earthly authority save their own conscience. Each travels their own path of atonement, appearing in villages like gentle storms–bringing healing, then vanishing before dawn. They avoid one another not from hostility, but from understanding that their penance must be walked in solitude, each carrying the weight of their own past without burdening others who share the same burden.

#### The Leeches

Yet even in solitude, the Firebird’s original guidance calls to them. Each Kaithry feels an inexplicable pull toward the life they abandoned—the path they should have walked before choosing Hedon’s rebellion. A former farmer’s son finds himself drawn to rural communities, tending livestock diseases with intimate knowledge born from childhood memories. An abandoned blacksmith’s apprentice gravitates toward workshops, repairing both tools and the hands that wield them.

Working exclusively under cover of darkness, they demonstrate surgical precision that seems impossible for their apparent age. What witnesses mistake for exceptional skill and rare medicinal knowledge carries a deeper truth—their “herbal tinctures” and “blessed extracts” contain traces of their own vampiric blood. This essence not only accelerates physical healing but does something more profound: patients emerge stronger, more confident, more fundamentally themselves than they were even before injury struck. The shy find courage, the broken discover hope, the weak grow in strength.
##### Kryraic

Hedonites who pursue Kraic beyond all restraint eventually transcend mortality, gaining vampiric strength and speed. But with this dark gift comes devastating clarity—the ability to truly understand their victims’ suffering. These Kryraic become wandering contradictions, using their knowledge of how to harm in service of healing instead.
#### Algebra of Necesity

The Kaithry’s greatest gift becomes their most terrible vulnerability. Their vampiric blood regenerates constantly, sustaining them without need for feeding, yet this very essance that makes them self-sufficient can be their downfall. Each healing they perform draws from their life force, and while their supernatural nature replenishes what they give, the process has limits. Help too many, drain themselves too deeply, and the ancient hunger stirs.

Each moment of their life becomes an moral dilema, to heal more people and maintain their humanity or save a plague-stricken village and risk bringing out the Bloodworn Elder that they are trying so hard to repent for. Each act of mercy inches them closer to becoming the very evil they’ve spent centuries trying to atone for, yet they continue to act. For their penance is deserved and they know it better then anyone.

Becouse of their healing blood, they often have to constanlty run from not persecution but gratitude. When communities discover their miraculous abilities, gratitude transforms into desperate need, then into capture and exploitation. The Kaithry know that being bled daily by once–grateful villagers will eventually drain them past the breaking point and when they finally snap, those same villagers who loved them will become their victims. Better to vanish into the lonely night than risk becoming the monster that proves they never truly changed.

_**GM Note**_ Kaithry make an excellent recurring NPC, a mysterious healer who appears whenever needed only to vanish soon after the player party has been helped.

When communities discover a Kaithry’s true nature, the transition from gratitude to exploitation happens quickly—turning former allies into unwitting antagonists who genuinely believe they’re doing good by “sharing” the healer’s gift.
