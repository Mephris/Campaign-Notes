### Order of Hedon

_“Saving you? What the fuck are you on about?  
Give me something to drink, eat and a room to sleep in, or I’ll take your whole tavern instead. “_

Mercenary Captain Indulf Black

Like locust descending upon the fields, the Order of [[Hedon]] ravages both innocent and guilty alike, leaving devastation whenever they travel. These Knights or as they are more commonly called Ravagers, follow not a doctrine but an unclear philosophy, to seek pleasure no matter the consequence. Most of these Ravagers life their lives in the order without wondering too much about the true philosophy of their order, simply looking forward to next brutality they will be allowed to perform.

While their methods are unmoral to say the least, their efficiency is what separates them from mere bandits. While Carnelian knights guard of all the above the ground facilities and protections, Knights of [[Hedon]] are tasked within the shadows of the city, cleaning the drug cells, removing traitors and spies as well as keeping order in the lousily defined underground honor code.

#### Organization and Training

Unlike other Orders bound by rigid hierarchy and lengthy apprenticeships, the Order of [[Hedon]] operates on the radical principle of immediate personal sovereignty. Any individual interested in joining can declare themselves a knight. Though survival of their first solo contract determines whether that declaration holds meaning.

The Order draws recruits from those fighting their own nature: farmers destined for peaceful lives who crave battlefield glory, merchants’ children who despise commerce, blacksmiths who refuse the forge. What unites them isn’t philosophy but shared suffering—the constant emptiness of never feeling fulfilled by actions that go against their soul’s design. They seek in violence, excess, and cruelty the [[Kraic]] that their rebellious choices deny them.

New members receive no formal training beyond basic weapon maintenance and the Order’s core tenet: “Your choices are yours alone.” Learning occurs through observation and participation, with experienced knights taking on apprentices purely by personal preference. A recruit might shadow “Marcus Throat-Render” for months, learning interrogation techniques, or follow “Elena Poison-Tongue” through diplomatic missions that blur negotiation and blackmail.

Advancement happens through reputation rather than rank. Knights earn titles through personal achievements—“Gareth Coin-Counter” for extracting confessions through economic pressure, or “Sera Blood-Rose” for her signature crimson flowers left at assassination sites. These names carry more authority than formal designations, with leadership emerging naturally as successful knights attract followers seeking the same [[Kraic]].



#### Territory

The [[Hedon Order]] operates from the [[Hedon]] Quarter, a district that serves as both indulgence and isolation. Behind black iron gates marked with crimson banners, the sounds of revelry never cease—clash of practice weapons mingles with drunken laughter, while the scent of exotic spices masks darker odors. Unlike other Orders’ organized compounds, the Quarter resembles a permanent festival where pleasure and violence intertwine.

The district contains fighting pits where knights settle disputes through blood sport, taverns that never close serving drinks strong enough to numb spiritual emptiness, and chambers where information flows as freely as wine. Weapon smithies operate alongside gambling dens, while training yards border establishments offering more carnal distractions. This deliberate chaos reflects the Order’s philosophy—every pleasure available, every desire fulfilled, every void temporarily filled.

Beyond the Quarter, Hedon knights maintain no permanent outposts, instead establishing temporary camps wherever contracts take them. These mobile operations often convert their targets’ holdings into brief pleasure palaces, marked by the owner’s impaled corpse serving as both signature and warning. The Quarter remains their only true home, a place where knights can return between missions to lose themselves in the Kryraic.

##### Sanguine Addicts

Veterans of the Order grow increasingly hedonistic, their bodies building tolerance to common pleasures. Eventually, only the adrenaline rush of combat satisfies their cravings. The oldest members, known as the Bloodworn, remove their helmets in battle to bite fallen enemies, seeking the intoxicating taste of adrenaline-rich blood.
