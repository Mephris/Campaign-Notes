A bastard of the Varcró family from a maid and previous King as well as a half-brother of [[Zenrion Varcró]]. 

From young age sent out onto battlefield, he was the most zelous follower of the Phenix, knowing the [[Tenets of Grahaim | Tenets]] and [[Tale of the Firebird]] by heart, taught by his [[Valkyr|grandfather]].

He formed his own force within the [[Grahaim Order |Order of Grahaim]], accepting Gaelkoblood in early days. 

The most zelous of all Phenixes follower pushed though all the assasination attempts, purged the wild tribes while reading of [[Saint Archduke Solomon Grahaim]] deeds, ripped though the fey trickery and flesh until one memorable day when during his 29th age, he was deployed at the same field as the [[22nd Saint]]. 

![[Learog.png]]

He fought harder then before, slayed more fey then the Saint of [[Tale of The Last Cycle/Entities/Aspects/Aspects of Grael/Conflict|Firebird]] trying to impress the young woman. They fought at first shoulder to shoulder, until Learog pushed further and further leaving the object of his faith in the back knowing she would never too far behind him.

A massacre in the name of the Firebird occured as Learog pushed to a gathering of fey, there in the midst of corpses meeting a young Aov Sil woman he named Cathrine. While behind him the saint looked in horror at what had occured. 

Seeing the Saint of an object of his worship afraid, he grew in confusion and fell his guard. Not noticing a deceptive fey in the bushes next to the Saintess. There, did the 22nd sain fall, afraid of her own people just as much of the enemy, and so she flew, far, as far as she could. 

While Learog stayed back... with realization that his own divinity... abandoned its people. 

Afraid for the first time, he looked around... the flames of firebird within him diminishing. As he had made their god abandon him. 

He understood... his father was right to wish his death. His blade moved to his neck and would pierce it if not for a small hand upon his hand and afraid eyes of Aov Sil woman. 

He then moved back, back onto his land with a lie and a promise. 
That he was blessed as the next Saint... and a promise that he would never abandon his people as she did.
