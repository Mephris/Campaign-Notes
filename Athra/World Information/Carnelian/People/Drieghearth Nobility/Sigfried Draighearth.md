The [[Bogóir]] of the Red Scale
![[Sigfried_Drieghearth.png]]
While Bogóir are almost extinct, some still roam Lethrai in their small (human sized forms). 

One such was the Bogóir of the Red Scale, Sigfried later called [[Draighearth]], a son of a witch learned in the old ways of war and the [[War of Unmaking]].

It was him that followed [[Saint Archduke Solomon Grahaim|Solomon Grahaim]] as equal, passing the old stories and lessons before making them into organized art of war to be used by the next generations. 

While given a name [[Draighearth]] and a land of Carnelian above the [[Light's End Tomb]] he never truly fit into the definition of simple noble. Continuing his Dragon Hunts, bringing in the young as slaves and locking them in the walls of the [[Light's End Tomb|Light's End]] so they may never fly with the cursed wings of theirs. 

In the end his fiery temper and many many heroic stories brought him the attention of a [[Storyteller]], which ended in him being sealed as a Fable within her library.

The Kingdom however was far from safe, as the Elven and Fae races continued to push back with more and more armies clashing against Cold Iron equipped Knights. It was then a young squire Siegfrid came before the Archduke himself. It is not known what was said in detail, though witnesses recall the young squire lecturing the Archduke as if he were scolding a wayward child. 
> Solomon, while amused by the man’s bold character, could not ignore such insubordination before his court. The young knight was sentenced to death by service–a fate that awaited most Carnelians in these times of war anyway–granted a rank of a strategist and low ranking commander of Carnelian knights. Siegfried the Brave led a small group of archers to the Fae controlled fields. His guerrilla tactics turned the very ground the southern forest fae had shifted against them, allowing archers to slowly push the borders back to the fae forest and even into enemy territory. In year 45th of the Third age Knight Commander Siegfrid aggressively negotiated a cease fire with the Fae monarchs who agreed to a decade long ceasefire. Granted a rank of a noble and a name of Draighearth he traveled west to deal with the remaining of Centaur tribes and form a steady front against their rising threat."
