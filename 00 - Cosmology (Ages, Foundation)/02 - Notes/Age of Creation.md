# Age of Creation

Before the beginning, there was the end, and from the end [[Tuatha|chaos]] spilled.

Flooding empty in its unformed darkness. 

Stillwater drowned in screaming vigil, the form unformed, drowned and lone. 

From rippling screams... a strand was formed, binding all of the unformed.  

Thus, did all began, The [[Tuatha|Dark of Depth]], and the [[Grael|Light of Form]].

[[Grael]], the first light, took her form, she made of it pale-earth, still-winds, ever-flames and deep-waters. She defined her rules and phenomena. Making a world pale, quiet and stagnant.

[[Tuatha]] took its inks, painting a dress of shifting colors and mists, and as the first lights of [[Grael]] fell upon the world, life was formed.

## The Dawn
---
### Hatching of Wildern
Upon the first meeting of light and form, when the first root took hold, was born the first [[12 - Aspects|Aspect]] of [[Grael]] — [[Wildern]], she was born of air gathering onto feathers, the she-owl, and she was made of white strands like an empty canvas. 

In her birth did she pull the strands of the world, giving it form, she sculptured powerful roots that melded the stone onto earth, progeny of her hand were the moving keepers, beasts of tasks meant for upkeep of her creation. 

So did the pale world exist, but it didn't live. Among all the movement, set in certainty there was no possibility, so did the perfect dead sculpture catch the eye of the [[Tuatha]] herself. 

### Flood of Beauty
At the finish of the iridescent dress, all the possibility, the blind eye fell upon the world of of [[Wildern]], empty of all but logic, and from the inks of the azure dress did [[Tuatha]] drink. Without a mouth, she sang forth soft chaos, the logic-less act. 

And the world was its audience, the soft whispery tones flew onto the howl of winds no longer following the design. The boom of her song did enter the deepest waters and so did ocean learn whisper, and the rivers howls, the rocks grounded by the shake of the song did grind and scrape — in all the song of the world, its first sounds and colors, was born the first [[12 - Aspects|Aspect]] of [[Tuatha]], [[Beauty]]. 

In her birth, did world break shackles of logic, and among all the reason... came meaning.

### The Flood & The Flight
So did the world came to be, but before the birth of [[Conflict]], her first dawn, days held no sun. They had no skylight to shine upon life, naught but the kindling lights hunted from the living woods and the soft shimmer of the luminous flora — and beast keepers of days lived with daylights, gathering and gnawing upon the lightly fruits. Hiding upon the first signs of inky song of [[Beauty]].

The Night flew like a flood of song, devouring all lights in its wake, leaving behind just the ringing song of elements, covering all things from the sight of the [[Wildern|She-Owl]]. From the lack of her sight, did the fruits of roots hide beyond the beastly lips. So the beastly keepers divide, ones, grew onto large forms, so they could rip apart the hiding fruits and devour it together with the roots. The others grew in fang and cunning, and they sought the hiding day beasts to feast on their flesh. 

In this sunless world, once bound by logic of [[Wildern]] vision, was now drowned in inky song. So she gathered her pure white wings and spread them upwards, flying above the the tainted world, upwards. Towards the cradle where once were [[Grael]] and [[Tuatha]] born.

#### [[Wildern]]'s Loom
Landing beyond the wrong note of [[Beauty]], in the middle of the pale stone cradle surrounded by jagged rocks she looked upon the greatest of them, jagged mountain and rose her spirit over it, the world shook as the [[12 - Aspects|Aspect]] called upon its [[11 - Primevals|Primeval]]. The world cried in pathetic defiance to its laws broken, the skies covered in flowing azure dress, as the jagged mountain was given vein, formed onto a beast of two legs, and its jagged form gripped onto the nearby  life, unraveling it upon strands like a loom, as red ink tainted the white stone. The taint of [[Tuatha]], little by little and body by body, dripping down from its surface onto the fertile pale ground.
